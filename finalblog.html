Voices for Change: Our Fight for Climate Justice

As young women living in a world increasingly affected by climate change, we recognize the unique challenges our generation faces. Each day, we witness the devastating impacts of environmental degradation—effects that disproportionately affect marginalized communities, especially women and girls. Carmela Ellaga, a 21-year-old from the Philippines, shares, “Growing up near the ocean, I saw how rising tides and storms disrupted my education and threatened my family’s way of life. My journey into environmental advocacy began when I learned about sustainable fishing practices. Today, I work to protect our marine resources and educate others in my community about conservation.” In Kenya, Ellyanne Wanjiku, just 10 years old, has already made significant strides. “After learning about the work of Wangari Maathai, I convinced my mother to help me plant a tree. Now, through my nonprofit, Children With Nature, we've planted over 250,000 trees! I believe that tree planting is essential for both our community and the planet.” Similarly, Xiye Bastida from the U.S. has turned her personal experiences into activism. “My hometown was devastated by flooding, forcing my family to flee. I want to ensure that future generations have the education and resources to combat climate change. We need climate education to be a fundamental right in schools!” Through our collective voices, we are determined to fight for climate justice. We advocate for policies that promote sustainability and equity, and we strive to inspire our peers to take action. Together, we can create a world where everyone, regardless of gender, can thrive amidst the challenges posed by climate change.
